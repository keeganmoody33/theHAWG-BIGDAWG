# ICP: Head of RevOps

RevOps leaders own the data quality and enrichment stack for the GTM org. They manage vendor budgets, evaluate data coverage, and care about CRM integration depth. They buy based on data match rate, cost per enriched record, and whether the tool reduces their vendor count.

This segment has the highest budget authority and longest sales cycle. The value prop is total cost of ownership: replacing 2-3 enrichment vendors with one API that has transparent metering (see [[hog-api-metering]]) and higher match rates.

## Key Points

- **Pain:** Managing 3+ enrichment vendors, each with different pricing models, data gaps, and integration requirements
- **Decision criteria:** Data match rate, cost per record, CRM integration, vendor consolidation potential
- **Buying signal:** Currently paying for ZoomInfo + Apollo + Clearbit, or evaluating enrichment stack changes
- **Disqualifier:** Company with <10 SDRs (enrichment volume too low to justify vendor evaluation)
- **Messaging angle:** "One enrichment API replacing your three-vendor stack — transparent credits, higher match rates"
